ABOUT ME

I have an addiction for Indian motocycles

My fascination for Indian motocycles started as a young kid playing with diecast models and watching war movies. My first Indian motorcycle was a 2001 Indian Scout (Gilroy) followed by the 1999 Limited Edition Indian Chief (Gilroy).

My Indian motorcycle addiciton became obvious after purchasing my first pre-1953 "real" Indian motocycle, a restored 1943 WWII Indian Scout 741. I became fascinated by the entire 100+ year history of Indian Motocycles. Next, I wanted an older Indian Chief. Since I couldn't afford to purchase another fully restored Indian, I decided to try to build one from scratch. I found a great 1946 Indian Chief frame. While looking for a 1946 motor, I found a rare 1944 Indian Chief frame *with* a motor. Rather than focusing on one at a time, I became obsessed with finding the rest of the parts in pairs. When I'm not programming or riding, I'm learning how to wrench on these old bikes!

The best part of the "finding parts" journey, is meeting other folks with Indian addictions. Many of these folks turn you onto other leads. That is exactly how I found my new 1939 Indian Chief via a friend of a friend.

Fair warning: This addiction is contagious. Proceed with caution :)
